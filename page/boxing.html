Boxing Fundamentals

Master the heavy bag and sharpen your skills

By Marcus Vancity | 2 days ago

Mastering the heavy bag is about more than just hitting hard. It’s about rhythm, rotation, and maintaining your defensive integrity while delivering maximum power.

1. Stance and Balance

Your power starts from the ground up. Ensure your feet are shoulder-width apart, with your lead foot pointing towards the target and your back foot at a 45-degree angle. Keep your weight centered.

2. The Jab: Your Most Important Weapon

A good jab sets everything else up. It should be fast, snappy, and return to your chin immediately. Don't just push the bag; snap your punch.

3. Rotating Through the Core

Real power comes from the rotation of your hips and core, not just your arms. When throwing a cross or a hook, pivot on your feet and let your whole body follow through.

Pro Tip:

Always keep your eyes on the target. Don't look at the floor or away from the bag when you're throwing combinations.
