The Electrolyte Equation

Why water isn't always enough for elite performance

By Dr. Elena Kostic | 1 week ago

When you train at high intensity, you lose more than just water. Electrolytes—minerals that carry an electric charge—are vital for muscle function and hydration balance.

Sodium: The Hydration Leader

Sodium is the primary electrolyte lost in sweat. It helps maintain fluid balance and is crucial for nerve signaling and muscle contraction. Without enough sodium, you risk cramping and fatigue.

Potassium and Magnesium

Potassium works alongside sodium to manage cellular fluid levels, while magnesium plays a key role in muscle relaxation and energy production. A deficiency in either can lead to muscle tremors or weakness.

The Perfect Balance

For workouts lasting longer than 60 minutes, plain water often isn't enough. Look for electrolyte mixes that provide a balanced ratio of sodium, potassium, and magnesium to keep your performance steady.

Warning Signs:

If you experience dizziness, extreme fatigue, or persistent cramping, it's a sign that your electrolyte levels are dangerously low. Rehydrate immediately with a balanced solution.
