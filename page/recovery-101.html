Recovery 101

Why rest days are vital for growth and performance

By Marcus Vancity | 5 days ago

You don't grow in the gym; you grow while you sleep. Recovery is the most overlooked aspect of fitness, yet it's the most critical for long-term progress.

The Importance of Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep. This is when your body releases Growth Hormone (GH) and repairs the micro-tears in your muscle tissues.

Active Recovery

A rest day doesn't mean staying in bed all day. Light movement like walking, yoga, or swimming can help flush out lactic acid and keep joints mobile.

Nutrition and Hydration

Recovery requires nutrients. Ensure you're hitting your protein targets and staying hydrated to facilitate the transportation of nutrients to your muscles.

Warning Signs:

Chronic fatigue, persistent soreness, and a drop in mood are signs of overtraining. Listen to your body and take an extra rest day when needed.
