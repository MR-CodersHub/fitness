Fueling for Endurance

Optimize your nutrient intake for longer training sessions

By Sarah Miller | 3 days ago

Whether you're training for a marathon or a high-intensity CrossFit session, what you eat can make or break your performance. Endurance training requires a specific approach to fueling.

Pre-Workout: The Carb Load

Complex carbohydrates are your best friend. Aim for slow-releasing energy sources like oats, brown rice, or sweet potatoes 2-3 hours before your session.

Intra-Workout: Staying Fueled

For sessions longer than 90 minutes, consider easily digestible carbs like gels or electrolytes to maintain glycogen levels.

Post-Workout: Repair and Replenish

A 3:1 ratio of carbohydrates to protein is ideal for recovery. This helps replenish glycogen stores while providing amino acids for muscle repair.

Hydration Rule:

Don't wait until you're thirsty to drink. Sip water consistently throughout your workout to avoid dehydration and cramping.
